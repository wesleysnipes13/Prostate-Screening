About This Resource

A Smarter Way to Approach Prostate Cancer Screening

Prostate cancer is the most common non-skin cancer in American men. The PSA blood test can help detect it early — but screening is not right for everyone, and navigating the results can be confusing. This site guides you through the evidence so you can make an informed decision alongside your physician.

PSA 101

What Is a PSA Test?

PSA (Prostate-Specific Antigen) is a protein produced by prostate cells. A simple blood test measures how much PSA is circulating in your bloodstream. PSA levels naturally rise with age and can be elevated by a variety of conditions — not just cancer.

An elevated PSA does not mean you have cancer. It is a signal that warrants further evaluation and discussion with your physician. Many men with elevated PSA levels turn out not to have prostate cancer.

💡 The Key Principle

PSA screening is a conversation starter, not a verdict. This site will help you understand what your number means and what to ask your doctor.
